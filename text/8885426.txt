Total amounts of arsenic in foods sold in Croatia were determined over a 5-year period ( 1988-1993 ) .
Highest mean levels were recorded in the following food groups : fish ( 498 micrograms/kg ) , fishery products ( 270 micrograms/kg ) and cheese and dairy products ( 39 micrograms/kg ) ; lowest levels were contained in fruit , chocolate and fruit products ( 0.2 , 0.2 and 0.3 microgram/kg , respectively ) .
Mean weekly dietary arsenic intake estimated on the basis of the level of arsenic and consumption of the studied foodstuffs is 81.9 micrograms/individual .
The PTWI level achieved by the general population of Croatia is 7.8% .
