Urinary metals were characterized in 57 male residents of a small-scale gold mining community in Ghana .
Chromium and arsenic exceeded health guideline values for 52% and 34% , respectively , of all participants .
About 10-40% of the participants had urinary levels of aluminum , copper , manganese , nickel , selenium , and zinc that fell outside the U.S. reference range .
Exposures appear ubiquitous across the community as none of the elements were associated with occupation , age , and diet .
